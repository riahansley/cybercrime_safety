Protect Yourself from Identity Theft

Learn what identity theft is and how to keep your information safe.

What Is It? Why It's Dangerous Stay Safe Tips

What Is Identity Theft?

Identity theft is when someone steals your personal information and pretends to be you. This can include your name, birthday, address, passwords, or even your online accounts. Criminals often use this information to access your accounts, buy things, or trick other people while pretending to be you.

Why Is Identity Theft Dangerous?

Identity theft is dangerous because someone using your information can cause serious problems. They could hack into your social media, change your account passwords, spend money from your accounts, or even send messages pretending to be you. This can damage your reputation, cause stress, and take a long time to fix.

How Can You Stay Safe?

  • Keep your passwords private and never share them.
  • Use strong passwords with numbers, letters, and symbols.
  • Donâ€™t post personal details like your address or full birthday online.
  • Be careful what you share on social media.
  • Use two-factor authentication for extra security.
  • Tell a trusted adult if something feels strange or unsafe online.

What To Do If You Think Your Identity Was Stolen

If you think someone has used your information, tell a teacher, parent, or trusted adult right away. Change your passwords immediately, check your accounts for strange activity, and report the issue to your school or the website involved.
