Stay Safe from Phishing

Learn what phishing is and how to protect yourself online.

Learn More Stay Safe Tips Why It's Dangerous

What Is Phishing?

Phishing is when someone tries to trick you into giving away personal information, like passwords or login details. They pretend to be a real company or person you trust. These fake messages can come as emails, texts, or websites that look real but are designed to fool you.

Why Is Phishing Dangerous?

Phishing is dangerous because scammers can use the information you give them to break into your accounts. They might steal your money, read private messages, or pretend to be you. Some phishing links can even put harmful software on your device, causing even more problems.

How Can You Stay Safe?

  • Do not click on strange or unexpected links.
  • Check the sender’s email address carefully.
  • Never share your passwords with anyone.
  • Watch for spelling mistakes or messages that try to scare you.
  • If unsure, ask a trusted adult or teacher for help.
  • Use strong passwords and turn on two-factor authentication.

What To Do If You Think You’ve Been Phished

If you think you clicked a phishing link or gave away information, tell a parent, teacher, or trusted adult immediately. Change your passwords right away and report the message so others won’t fall for the same trick.
